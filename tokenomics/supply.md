# 📊 Token Supply

$TROVE has a fixed, intentionally scarce supply. No mint function exists — the supply is set at deployment and can only decrease from that point forward.

---

## Supply at a Glance

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| **Total Supply** | 100,000 TROVE |
| **Mintable** | No — fixed at deployment |
| **Burnable** | Yes — burned tokens are permanently removed |
| **Effective Supply** | Decreases over time, never increases |

---

## Why 100,000?

100,000 TROVE is scarce by design. With a small fixed supply, every burn and every ETH deposit into the Vault has an **immediate, visible impact** on the floor price.

With 100,000 TROVE and 1 ETH in the Vault:
```
Floor = 1 ETH ÷ 100,000 = 0.00001 ETH per TROVE
```

After 10,000 TROVE burned from buybacks:
```
Floor = 1 ETH ÷ 90,000 = 0.0000111 ETH per TROVE  (+11%)
```

Every burn is felt. There's no dilution hiding behind an inflated supply.

---

## Supply Trajectory

Supply only ever decreases through:

1. **Mining buybacks** — ASIC revenue buys TROVE from LP and sends it to the dead address
2. **Vault sales (DApp)** — holders sell TROVE to the Vault at floor price; tokens are permanently burned
3. **No new issuance** — no team allocation, no staking rewards, no future mints

---

## Supply vs Floor Price

| Circulating Supply | Vault ETH | Floor Price |
|---|---|---|
| 100,000 | 1 ETH | 0.00001 ETH |
| 80,000 | 1.5 ETH | 0.00001875 ETH |
| 50,000 | 2.5 ETH | 0.00005 ETH |
| 20,000 | 4 ETH | 0.0002 ETH |

As supply compresses and ETH accumulates from swap fees and mining revenue, the floor price per token grows significantly. Early holders benefit most from staying through the compression.
